How it's made

Stencil. Cut. Glow.

Stencil Lit
↔
1

Print & pin

The logo is sized, printed, and pinned to a carvable foam pumpkin — fringe-cut so it hugs the curve without distorting a single letter.

2

Score & carve

Every line is poked through, then carved by hand. Some shapes are cut clean through; others are shaved thin so they glow softer than the rest.

3

Light it up

A light drops inside and the logo wakes up — crisp by day, radiant by night. Drag the slider to watch a stencil become a sign.

The two things in every carve

The tool

Dremel cordless rotary

Every line you see is cut with a Dremel hand carver — a cordless rotary tool that spins fine bits fast enough to shave a foam pumpkin like butter. It's what lets a serif stay a serif: thin enough to glow, deep enough to read, with control a knife can't touch.

  • Variable speed for fine micro-type
  • Cuts clean-through and shallow relief
  • Cordless — no cable dragging across the carve
The canvas

Hide & Eeek carvable pumpkins

The blanks are Hide & Eeek faux carvable pumpkins — Target's seasonal foam pumpkins. They take a Dremel without rotting, hold a crisp edge for weeks, and glow an even amber when lit from within. Stocked in-store only through the season, so the carving calendar runs on Target's.
